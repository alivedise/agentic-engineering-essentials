# [AEE-101] The Agentic Capability Gap

## Context

There is a growing and widely misunderstood gap in AI capability perception. Engineers who form their views of AI from free-tier chat interfaces, older models, or casual use cases are working from an outdated and unrepresentative sample. This creates two groups speaking past each other: one dismissing AI as a novelty that fumbles simple questions, and one that has witnessed frontier agentic models autonomously restructure codebases over hours.

Understanding this gap is essential for making sound engineering decisions about when and how to invest in agentic systems.

## Design Think

AI capability is not uniform. It is stratified by:

1. **Model tier** -- free/deprecated models vs. frontier paid models differ dramatically in reasoning depth, instruction following, and agentic reliability
2. **Task domain** -- technical, verifiable domains (programming, math, formal reasoning) have improved far faster than soft domains (writing, advice, search) due to the availability of verifiable reward signals for reinforcement learning
3. **Mode of use** -- a model answering a one-shot question is a different system than a model given a terminal, long-horizon goals, and hours of runtime

Engineers MUST calibrate their expectations to the specific model tier, domain, and usage mode they are targeting. Generalizing from one configuration to another leads to systematic misjudgments.

The practical implication: if you have not yet used a frontier agentic model (e.g., Claude Code, OpenAI Codex) on a real engineering task with a real codebase, your mental model of AI capability is likely significantly out of date as of 2025-2026.
