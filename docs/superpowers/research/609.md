## Research notes

**Major scope finding — flag for the writer.** The brief's framing of ACP as "the peer of A2A under Linux Foundation, REST-native vs A2A's JSON-RPC-first" is now historically true but no longer current. On August 29, 2025, the Linux Foundation announced that ACP "joined forces with" A2A under LF AI & Data, and the BeeAI/ACP team has formally been winding down ACP active development and contributing its work into A2A. As of April 2026, the canonical ACP domain (agentcommunicationprotocol.dev) and the `i-am-bee/acp` GitHub repo both display the merger banner ("ACP is now part of A2A under the Linux Foundation!") and direct users to migration guides. Kate Blair (IBM) sits on A2A's TSC alongside Google, Microsoft, AWS, Cisco, Salesforce, ServiceNow, and SAP representatives.

This means AEE-609 cannot be written as a "current peer of A2A" article in good faith. The article framing the writer should adopt:

1. Treat ACP as a **historically important, now-merged** protocol whose distinctive design contributions (REST-native, first-class Sessions, multi-modal MessageParts with MIME types, Await for human-in-the-loop, online+offline discovery) are being absorbed into A2A.
2. Keep the article — it is still valuable to teach the design choices ACP made — but be explicit that for new projects in 2026 the practical recommendation is "use A2A; understand ACP for context and for legacy interop." Reference the LF announcement and BeeAI migration guide.
3. The session model contrast against A2A's task-centric model remains a useful pedagogical point, since A2A is now expected to absorb ACP's stateful-session ideas via TSC discussions; mention this absorption is in progress.

**Spec version.** No primary source surfaced an explicit version number for ACP (e.g. v1.0). The spec was effectively frozen at the point of the A2A merger; for a published article, "ACP at the time of the August 2025 merger" is the most defensible phrasing.

**Auth model.** Primary ACP docs are notably thin on a formal authentication scheme — by design ACP delegates auth to standard HTTP machinery (TLS, bearer tokens, etc.) rather than mandating a particular scheme. Avoid claiming ACP has a dedicated auth model. The WorkOS article's "capability tokens" claim is unverified and contradicts primary sources; rejected.

**Adopters.** Verified named integrators are sparse. Beyond IBM/BeeAI itself, public BeeAI documentation lists Aider and GPT-Researcher as available agents; LangChain and CrewAI are mentioned by IBM blog as frameworks ACP intends to bridge, not as confirmed adopters. Recommend keeping any "adoption" language to "BeeAI framework, with bridging guidance for LangChain and CrewAI" and avoid naming enterprise adopters that cannot be primary-sourced.

**Disambiguation confirmed.** This article correctly targets the IBM/BeeAI ACP. The arXiv "Beyond Context Sharing" ACP paper is a separate academic proposal and was excluded. AGNTCY's "Agent Connect Protocol" did not appear in canonical IBM/BeeAI/LF docs, so no merger with AGNTCY occurred — the only merger in scope is ACP into A2A.
